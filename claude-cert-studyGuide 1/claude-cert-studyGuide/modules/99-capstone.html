4. Objection Handling

Prepare responses for 5 common objections you might hear from the CTO. Write your response first, then reveal the example response to compare.

"AI will replace our employees"

Example: "Claude is designed to augment your team, not replace them. Think of it as giving every employee a highly capable assistant. In practice, companies that deploy Claude see their employees handling higher-value work -- customer support agents resolve complex issues instead of answering FAQs, developers build features instead of writing boilerplate. The goal is to make your 2,000 employees as productive as 3,000, not to reduce headcount to 1,000."

"We are concerned about data security"

Example: "Data security is our top priority as well. Anthropic's enterprise offering includes SOC 2 Type II compliance, zero data retention on API calls -- your data is never used for training. We can deploy with your existing SSO, enforce data classification policies, and set up audit logging. We recommend starting with a pilot using only non-sensitive data, then expanding as you verify the security posture meets your requirements."

"How do we measure ROI?"

Example: "We will establish clear baseline metrics before deployment -- average ticket resolution time, developer velocity, content production throughput. During the pilot (first 2 weeks, 10 users), we measure these same metrics with Claude and calculate the delta. Most enterprise clients see 3-5x ROI within the first quarter. We will build a dashboard tracking time saved per user, cost per AI-assisted task, and quality metrics so you have real-time visibility."

"Our team will not adopt it"

Example: "Adoption is about making the tool feel like a natural part of their workflow, not an extra step. We start with your most enthusiastic team -- the ones already experimenting with AI. They become champions who demonstrate real wins. We provide role-specific training (not generic 'AI 101') and focus on immediate, tangible time savings. The key is showing someone that the task they dreaded (writing that report, reviewing that code, answering those emails) now takes 10 minutes instead of 2 hours."

"What if Claude gives wrong answers?"

Example: "This is exactly the right question to ask. Claude can make mistakes, just like any tool or employee. The mitigation strategy is layered: First, Claude is trained to say 'I don't know' rather than guess -- it has the best calibration of any AI model. Second, for high-stakes outputs, we implement human-in-the-loop review. Third, we use tool-grounding -- Claude retrieves facts from your knowledge base rather than relying on training data. Fourth, we set up eval suites that continuously test accuracy. The goal is not perfection; it is being measurably better than the status quo."
